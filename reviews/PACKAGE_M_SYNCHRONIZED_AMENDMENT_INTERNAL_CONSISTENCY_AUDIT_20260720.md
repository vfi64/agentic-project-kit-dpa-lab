## 4. DPA-200 ↔ DPA-300 consistency

Result: `PASS` subject to IC-m01.

The package preserves:

- inherited helper paths without independent contract proliferation;

No second registry, writer, lifecycle, state store or persistence authority is introduced.

### IC-m01 — Generated-surface repair wording permits an unsafe reading

Severity: minor.

The shared generated-edit-surface definition and DPA-200 candidate language use formulations such as `regenerate or re-accept ... as applicable` or `regeneration or re-acceptance before accepted use` after direct emergency repair.

A direct byte repair creates target mismatch and cannot qualify for DPA-500 gate-set-only re-acceptance. Leaving `re-acceptance` unqualified could be read as permitting repaired generated bytes to become accepted without regeneration.

Required correction:

- direct repair remains temporary and non-accepted;
- acceptance requires governed regeneration from canonical authority;
- mutation-free re-acceptance remains available only where DPA-500 §15.6 independently permits it and therefore cannot legitimize changed target bytes.

### IC-m02 — Aggregate recovery identity requires an explicit existing-authority fence

Severity: minor.

The draft says aggregate failure creates or records one bounded orchestration recovery identity. ADR-022 requires aggregate recovery to use existing workflow or lifecycle recovery structures and forbids a new recovery store or owner.

Although that boundary is present elsewhere, the insertion-ready DPA-300 recovery candidate should state it directly where the identity is introduced.

Required correction:

- the aggregate recovery identity must be represented only through an existing validated workflow or lifecycle recovery structure;
- it must not establish an independent store, owner, trust state, acceptance state or canonical history.

## 8. Changed-path consistency
