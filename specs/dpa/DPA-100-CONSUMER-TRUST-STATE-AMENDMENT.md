# DPA-100 — Consumer Trust-State Vocabulary Amendment

Status: stable
Status-date: 2026-07-15
Owner: `specs/dpa/DPA-100-FOUNDATIONS.md`
Decision: DPA-ADR-014

## 1. Authority and precedence

This amendment registers the consumer trust-state dimension under DPA-100 vocabulary ownership.

Until mechanically folded into `DPA-100-FOUNDATIONS.md`, this amendment has precedence over the statement that DPA-ADR-009 separates only four dimensions.

It does not alter the meanings of repository classifications, document statuses, progress statuses or access outcomes.

## 2. Fifth closed vocabulary dimension

Consumer trust state is a fifth closed vocabulary dimension for generated/projected bytes:

- `computed`;
- `plan-captured`;
- `written-unverified`;
- `accepted`;
- `abandoned`.

DPA-200 owns the architectural meanings and transitions of these states. DPA-300 owns lifecycle transitions. DPA-500 owns gate consequences and explicit acceptance invalidation.

## 3. Collision rule

The document-status token `planned` MUST NOT be used as a consumer trust state.

No consumer trust-state token may be reused with a different meaning in another closed status dimension.

## 4. Dimension separation

A consumer trust state MUST NOT be combined with repository classification, document status, progress status or access outcome into an undeclared compound status.

Examples:

- a DPA specification may have document status `review-ready` while describing projected bytes in trust state `plan-captured`;
- a review may have access outcome `accessible` and verdict `ACCEPT_WITH_CHANGES`; neither is a trust state;
- a repository fact may be `VERIFIED` while generated bytes remain `written-unverified`.

## 5. Conformance

Later specifications MUST use the exact trust-state tokens registered here and MUST NOT introduce additional trust states without an accepted decision and synchronized DPA-100 amendment.
