## Invalid-state catalog mapping

DPA-200 owns the normative invalid-state catalog. Planned negative tests map to:

1. missing or ambiguous canonical authority;
2. ambiguous target identity;
3. overlapping regions;
4. unowned bytes;
5. duplicate ownership;
6. missing, duplicated, malformed or shared-control boundaries;
7. renderer-produced partition bytes;
8. manual or historical partition mutation;
9. undeclared semantic input;
10. evidence or history used as runtime authority;
11. premature acceptance;
12. automatic historical merge;
13. unavailable or invented rollback source;
14. silent malformed-contract fallback;
15. one invocation producing multiple registered targets;
16. production-form selection without DP1 evidence;
17. any parallel registry, lifecycle, freshness, evidence, Workspace or gate system.

## DP1 staging traceability

- Discovery: factual exact-ref inventory under ADR-015 and `integration/DP1_DISCOVERY_CONTRACT.md`.
- Probe: compatibility and sufficiency checks against reviewable contracts.
- Assessment: proof-of-architecture conclusion from Discovery and Probe evidence.

Discovery does not satisfy Probe or Assessment requirements.

## Main-repository validation boundary

The following remain `NEEDS_MAIN_REPO_VALIDATION`:

- registry support for region identity and partition representation;
- actual marker or structural-addressing conventions;
- actual lifecycle writer and direct-write paths;
- actual readers, writers and read order;
- actual finding, gate and severity mapping;
- actual atomic-write and crash-recovery behavior;
- actual cross-ref serialization mechanisms;
- actual rollback sources.

## Review readiness

DPA-200 traceability is review-ready when every form and invalid state maps to requirements, tests, later-spec ownership, evidence and rollback without claiming current implementation behavior.
