# Package G — Meta-Governance Validation and Adoption Plan

Status: active
Status-date: 2026-07-20

## 1. Purpose

Package G validates the Lab's working and development method before further substantive DPA work.

The package is intentionally prior to continued DPA architecture development. It treats governance itself as a falsifiable, maintainable and complexity-bounded system.

Reusable method authority is developed in the private repository:

`vfi64/agentic-governance-framework`

The Lab remains authoritative for its own adoption decision and DPA sequencing. The framework repository is not runtime authority for the Kit or the Lab.

## 2. Hard sequencing gate

Until Package G closes:

- no substantive new DPA normative package may begin;
- DPA-600 remains frozen;
- DPA-700 remains unstarted;
- existing safety or evidence preservation work may continue only when needed to prevent loss, correct a verified false claim or complete Package-G evidence;
- editorial and status synchronization may proceed when it does not alter DPA semantics.

Package M artifacts already produced remain review candidates and are not invalidated. Further architecture expansion is paused.

## 3. Governing framework baseline

The initial framework baseline consists of:

- `README.md`;
- `GOVERNANCE.md`;
- `METHOD.md`;
- `AUDIT_CONTRACT.md`;
- `ADOPTION_CONTRACT.md`;
- `profiles/agentic-project-kit.md`.

The exact framework ref must be frozen before the independent Package-G review.

## 4. Audit scope

The Lab must inventory and evaluate all active or materially influential instructions governing:

- bootstrap and authority order;
- execution and mutation boundaries;
- planning and roadmap sequencing;
- review, verification and adjudication;
- evidence and exact-ref claims;
- status vocabularies;
- repository access and capability diagnosis;
- handoff and continuity;
- operational hardening and later Kit import;
- Probe preparation and execution;
- architecture amendment and promotion;
- DPA/Kit authority separation.

Historical records need not be evaluated as active rules unless they still influence current work.

## 5. Required evaluation dimensions

Each rule or justified rule cluster must be assessed for:

1. correctness;
2. efficiency;
3. effectiveness;
4. redundancy freedom;
5. consistency;
6. completeness;
7. testability;
8. usability;
9. maintainability;
10. complexity proportionality.

The audit must determine not merely whether a rule exists, but whether it plausibly and observably produces the intended protection or result.

## 6. Complexity and outcome test

Governance may increase complexity only to improve one or more of:

- correctness;
- useful yield;
- safety;
- selectivity;
- maintainability;
- reproducibility;
- evidence quality.

Safety-critical false negatives remain a hard failure and may not be traded against cost or speed.

Every retained complex control must identify:

- protected risk;
- mechanism of action;
- simpler alternative considered;
- expected or observed benefit;
- cost and failure mode;
- later simplification or retirement trigger.

## 7. Required outputs

Package G must produce:

1. exact-ref framework baseline record;
2. Lab active-rule inventory;
3. rule authority and redundancy map;
4. correctness and consistency findings;
5. effectiveness and efficiency findings;
6. complexity-benefit findings;
7. simplification, consolidation and retirement candidates;
8. missing-control candidates;
9. independent review;
10. Maintainer adjudication;
11. synchronized Lab adoption record;
12. durable Kit adoption tracker;
13. Package-G closeout and DPA resume decision.

## 8. Review questions

The independent review must explicitly attempt to falsify:

- that the framework is more effective than the current Lab method alone;
- that added governance complexity is proportionate;
- that the audit can recommend deletion as readily as addition;
- that the framework avoids a second authority system;
- that the Kit adoption path cannot be silently forgotten;
- that the DPA freeze is bounded rather than indefinite.

## 9. Closeout criteria

Package G closes only when:

- the declared inventory is complete;
- every blocking finding is resolved or adjudicated;
- retained rules have justified purpose and authority;
- substantive redundancies are consolidated or explicitly retained with rationale;
- the framework baseline is accepted, amended or rejected explicitly;
- Lab adoption state is recorded;
- Kit adoption is tracked in the Kit repository;
- the Maintainer records an explicit `DPA_WORK_MAY_RESUME` or `DPA_WORK_REMAINS_BLOCKED` decision.

## 10. Later Kit adoption

The Kit must evaluate the framework against its then-current exact `main` ref and existing rule system.

The required outcome is not blind full adoption. Every applicable framework requirement must receive one of:

- adopt;
- adapt;
- already equivalent;
- superseded by an existing Kit control;
- not applicable;
- defer;
- reject.

No requirement may disappear without disposition, and no copied text may create a second independently evolving rule authority.
